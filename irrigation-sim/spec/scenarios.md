# Scenarios — Narratives & Reading Suggestions

## Scenario 1 — Weak Rules (Introductory)
Vignette:
"You are a household located in a small canal community in the Middle Gila valley during a period of stable flow. Canal cleaning happens informally and nobody enforces labor contributions."
Purpose: Demonstrate short-term upstream advantage and long-term system decay.
Recommended readings: Hohokam canal reconstructions (Woodson et al. and PMC article).

## Scenario 2 — Ritual & Reputation (Institutional)
Vignette:
"After repeated canal clogging, villages adopted an annual communal cleaning ritual and a labor schedule; reputation mattered and sanctions were rare but social."
Purpose: Show how ritual scheduling and reputation can enforce cooperation.
Recommended readings: Ethnographic analogies in American Antiquity piece and ethnographies of O'odham irrigation.

## Scenario 3 — Drought Stress Test (Resilience)
Vignette:
"Multi-year low flows stress the network. Large canal segments must be rebuilt if maintenance deficits persist. Archaeological reconstructions identify episodes consistent with canal destruction after extreme events."
Purpose: Test institutional resilience under repeated drought.
